Experimental Results

Task Environments

We utilize three diverse environments to evaluate the agent's alignment with reality, ranging from high-level planning to precise low-level control.

EB-ALFRED

Household Planning
Long-horizon task decomposition (e.g., "Slice & heat tomato") based on AI2-THOR.

EB-Habitat

Rearrangement
Object logistics tasks (e.g., "Move pear to sofa") built upon Habitat 2.0.

EB-Navigation

Low-Level Control
Precise atomic actions (e.g., "Move 0.25m") requiring strict spatial reasoning.

Below are the detailed performance comparisons on the EB-ALFRED and EB-Habitat environments, along with ablation studies. Click the tabs to explore.

Performance on EB-ALFRED

Table 1: Main Results on the EB-ALFRED Environment. Comparison of Success Rate (SR) and Goal Condition (GC). WorldMind achieves superior performance in tasks requiring long-horizon planning.

Key Analysis:

  • Superior Success Rate: With GPT-4.1-mini, WorldMind achieves an SR of 50.0%, significantly outperforming ReAct (41.6%).
  • Improved Process Adherence: The Goal Condition (GC) score improves to 50.8%, indicating better sub-goal completion.
  • Visual Robustness: Shows remarkable stability in the "Visual Appearance" subset.

Performance on EB-Habitat

Table 2: Main Results on the EB-Habitat Environment. Evaluation on five subsets: Base, Common, Complex, Visual, and Spatial.

Key Analysis:

  • Consistent Gains: WorldMind outperforms the ReAct baseline by +9.2% SR on GPT-4.1-mini.
  • Safety & Feasibility: Significant improvement in Spatial Awareness demonstrates effective physical filtering.
  • Error Reduction: Drastic reduction in "Invalid Action" errors compared to baselines.

Ablation Study

Table 3: Impact of Goal Guidance and Process Supervision. Isolating the contributions of Goal Experience and Process Experience.

Key Analysis:

  • Goal Experience: Primarily enhances the Goal Condition (GC) metric, preventing navigational drift.
  • Process Experience: Drives improvements in Success Rate (SR) by acting as a physical safeguard.
  • Synergy: The full model performs best, confirming the need for both heuristic guidance and physical verification.

Low-Level Navigation Tasks

Table 4: Generalization to Low-Level Control (EB-Navigation). Evaluating control of atomic actions (e.g., "Rotate 90 degrees").

Key Analysis:

  • Granularity Generalization: WorldMind effectively bridges high-level reasoning and low-level execution (+10.0% for GPT-3.5).
