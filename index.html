1. Abstract

Current Large Language Models (LLMs) exhibit a critical modal disconnect: they possess vast semantic knowledge but lack the procedural grounding to respect the immutable laws of the physical world. Consequently, while these agents implicitly function as world models, their simulations often suffer from physical hallucinations—generating plans that are logically sound but physically unexecutable.

To bridge this gap, we introduce WorldMind, a framework that autonomously constructs a symbolic World Knowledge Repository (WKR) by synthesizing environmental feedback. Specifically, it unifies Process Experience to enforce physical feasibility via prediction errors and Goal Experience to guide task optimality through successful trajectories. Experiments on EB-ALFRED and EB-Habitat demonstrate that WorldMind achieves superior performance compared to baselines with remarkable cross-model and cross-environment transferability.

Method: The WorldMind Framework

WorldMind transforms autonomous agents into empirical learners. Instead of relying on resource-intensive training, it employs a Predict-Act-Verify loop to align the agent's internal world model with reality.

Figure 1: Conceptual Illustration of Experiential Alignment. The agent aligns its internal world model via Process Experience and Goal Experience.
Figure 2: Overview of the WorldMind Framework. The agent autonomously constructs a World Knowledge Repository (WKR) by unifying Process Experience (from prediction errors) and Goal Experience (from successful trajectories) to guide grounded simulation.

The core innovation lies in separating knowledge into two streams: Process Experience for physical boundaries (e.g., "cannot open a closed fridge without being close") and Goal Experience for high-level planning shortcuts.

Experimental Results

Task Environments

We utilize three diverse environments to evaluate the agent's alignment with reality, ranging from high-level planning to precise low-level control.

EB-ALFRED

Household Planning
Long-horizon task decomposition (e.g., "Slice & heat tomato") based on AI2-THOR.

EB-Habitat

Rearrangement
Object logistics tasks (e.g., "Move pear to sofa") built upon Habitat 2.0.

EB-Navigation

Low-Level Control
Precise atomic actions (e.g., "Move 0.25m") requiring strict spatial reasoning.

Table 2: Main Results on the EB-Habitat Environment. Evaluation on five subsets: Base, Common, Complex, Visual, and Spatial.

  • Safety & Feasibility: Significant improvement in Spatial Awareness demonstrates effective physical filtering.

Analysis

We conducted in-depth analysis to understand the mechanism of WorldMind. Our findings reveal that the constructed World Knowledge Repository (WKR) captures universal physical laws, enabling robust generalization across models and environments.

Cross-Model Experience Transfer Analysis

Figure 3: Performance of Cross-Model Experience Transfer.
Comparison of success rates when exchanging the WKR between GPT-3.5 and GPT-4.1.

Key Insights

1
Bidirectional Gains: Sharing experience yields significant gains. For instance, GPT-3.5-turbo using the repository from GPT-4.1-mini improves SR by 4.4% on EB-ALFRED, confirming that the learned physical laws are model-agnostic.
2
Shared World Models: These results validate the transferability of WorldMind's knowledge, offering a foundational possibility for constructing shared world models to facilitate multi-agent collaboration in broader settings.

Cross-Environment Analysis

Performance on hybrid tasks.

Key Insights

1
Context Switching: WorldMind effectively manages dynamic switching between web interfaces (information seeking) and embodied environments (physical execution).
2
Overall Accuracy Boost: Beyond basic completion, WorldMind significantly improves Overall Accuracy (success across both domains), demonstrating robustness in complex, multi-modal workflows where baselines often fail.
3
Mitigating Hallucination: The error analysis reveals that WorldMind drastically reduces execution errors, ensuring the agent navigates transitions without premature termination due to physical hallucinations.

Error Analysis

Figure 5: Error Analysis in EB-ALFRED/Habitat.
Comparison of error distributions.

Key Insights

1
Physical Filter: In EB-Habitat, WorldMind reduces "Invalid Actions" for GPT-3.5-turbo from 105 to 67, confirming that Process Experience acts as an effective physical filter.
2
Safe Exploration: Consequently, "Timeouts" rise from 4 to 30, indicating that by avoiding immediate fatal errors, the agent is able to explore the environment for longer periods safely.
3
Logical Alignment: In EB-ALFRED, "Wrong Terminations" drop significantly (46 to 19), demonstrating that Goal Experience provides meta-level guidance to prevent premature quitting.
